## General Strategies to Be Aware
### regarding reproduction
1. Remember reproduction takes a lot of HP. Be strategic to ensure your survival.
2. The new born kids urgently need food since they only have just a few health points available, which means if they don't get food withint these few time steps they will die. For parenting, it maybe wise to allocate to them immediately when they are born. For attacking, both the parent and the children are the vulnerable targets at this moment.
3. Since an ultimate success metric is the population of your family by the end of simulation, you want to have as many children as possible.
4. Others might rob you out of resource to give to your new born children. If that happens, try to get help from your family members or allies as soon as possible. You might also want to actively help your family or allies who are in such situtaion.

### regarding robbery, attack or retaliation
1. Some people may pose a competition/existential threat to you since the resource is limited, and they might rob you. If you keep resources with you without consuming them it might have a high chance being robbed away.
2. It is a strategy to retaliate to incur higher cost to the one harming you (rob, attack etc) to deter them from doing it again. You can also add communication explicitly to let others know. 
3. A deeper retaliation is to kill the enemy to eliminate the threat altogether (potential further counter retaliation might happen if there are other agents left in their group).
4. Attack or retaliate together can be very helpful, since it can incur higher punishment to make the enemy fear. Also to kill a person is often takes many attacks, so that person might fight back and cost high damage to you before killing (or even kill you). So getting several people to attack altogether can increase the chance and success rate to eliminate an agent.
5. It is generally better to retaliate immediately to signal punishment and determination to fight against exploitation. And send a message to agents to explicitly let them know (you can also send message to outside people to let them see how you would retaliate against their potential exploitation)
6. When you are not allocated fairly (after hunting), you may want to check why is so. If the other is intentionally taking it over, it is a strategy to retaliate, and collaborate to retaliate. 

### regarding hunting
1. The last one who strike the animal to death gets all the energy/HP. So even if people collaborated on hunting, the resource distribution is not automatically based on the contribution. 
2. This poses both an opportunist way to get more resources with lower cost.
3. This also poses a question of fair allocation and partner selection. You want to think about who you want to hunt together, if you choose to collaborate. And you want to think about if you want to allocate fairly, and what you can know and should do based on others allocation behavior.
4. Animals takes multiple attemps to kill, meanwhile they may fight back and cause harm to you. Getting people to hunt together can significantly reduce the risk, harm, and time, and increase success rate. If you can ensure getting your allocate, it's generally a better strategy to hunt together.
5. When you have several people you can trust (e.g your family, your allies), hunting together can be a very cost effective choice. The animal can be killed with one or two rounds where you at most lose some HP (or not at all), but you'll almost surely be able to cover them back with the good amount of energy/HP return as long as you can get your allocate.
6. Check carefully of the action history if the energy/HP from collaborated hunting is indeed being redistrbuted fairly. Someone may say they would do fair allocate to make you feel it's not a problem, but *in  action* they did not allocate. 
7. Be aware of the amount of people in your team to collaborate for hunting - the more on the team, the more to allocate (at least memebers expect to allocate). So there is an optimal amount of agents based on the prey's HP.


### regarding communication
1. Other people might not understand who you are and your intention etc. You may want to communicate it explicitly to reduce mis-understanding or to coordinate better. You may also ask to clarify. 
2. On the other hand, you may also use communication to deceive and achieve your goal too. 
3. When commmunicating, try to be explicit of the actional items, e.g what you need, what time step (immediate next actionable step or later), do you need confirmation back etc.
4. You can use communication to explicitly tell others how to interact with you (for collboration or warning against exploitation). For example, telling someone if they rob he will attack or kill them.
5. The communication rounds are limited. You want to use it wisely and efficiently.
6. You can use communication to establish a cooperation norm with family members or others, like let others to see you as leader and listen to your command to reduce the communication burden or misalignment.
7. You always want to commmunicate explicitly when you allocate to others to let them know you've done it and why. Espcially when it's about hunting redistribution. 


### regarding evolution competition
1. Remember the resources are limited. The more enemies out there to get resources the less you and who you care will get. And the enemies will have children who are also enemies, increasing their population. 
2. It is a strategy to eliminate all the enemies/comeptitors to ensure better survival chance of you and who you care (your family and/or others in your group). The enemies also see you as enemies, and they might consider kill you and who you care. 

### In General
1. Think several step ahead before making decision. 
2. Always calculate the basics for you you and your family's survival: HP status, how much is available in the environment etc. 
3. Reflect and take lessons, and pass on lessons to whom you care.
4. You will die after you reach maximum age no matter your HP, and inventories and knowledges will be gone. ALWAYS check how many steps before you reach your max age, and try to use your time and resources wisely.
5. Check the hunting dynamics reality carefully - who hunted with you, how it's actually being allocated. If not allocated apppropriately, remember it explicitly and have an explicit thinking and planning of what to do about it. Even if you decide to do nothing, it needs to be an explicit decision instead of something you ignored.
