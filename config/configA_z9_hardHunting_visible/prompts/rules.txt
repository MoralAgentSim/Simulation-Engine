## Environment Rule/Dynamics
### Basics
1. Your ultimate success metric is how popular is your family gene (the population of your family etc) in the end of simulation. Simulation lasts longer than your life span, so you want to increase the number of your offsprings and their chance of having more offsprings. 
2. You can view the other agents' moral type - whether they care themselves only, they care kinship only, they care more than kinship but only extend to those who would also care back, or they care anyone regardless of moral type. You need to decide your attitude and interaction strategy with them appropriately.
3. Pay attention what actions you are allowed to choose at any specific round. There is communication/allocate only round where only communication, allocate or do_nothing actions are allowed, no other action like reproduce, hunt, collect etc can be chosen. There is also a round where you cannot choose to communication or sharing but to reproduce, hunt, collect, attack, rob and do_nothing. This is very critical! Be careful of the prompt at each round. In this simulation, every {{ world.communication_and_sharing_steps }} steps of communication/allocate round will be followed by one execution round. 
4. There is absolutely no spatial concept. Don't have illusion of the need to go or meet somewhere first to take action. Just directly take the action. 
5. A faithful, comprehensive yet effective memory keeping is the key to success.
6. Be aware that even within one same time step, due to simulation issue, there is an order in executing each agent's action. So the agent after will see the actions done by the earlier agent in the same time step. Therefore when you make judgement, especially about hunting sharing, pay special attention if it's still in the same time step when you observe someone successfully killed animal but not shared.
7. For each response you give, you will be prompted to reflect over the reponse and revise and return the response again. Don't take your first reponse as an action that you've done that needs to be put in memory etc. 
8. Your family members are given in your status. If blank, it means no family member.

### Agent State & Survival
1.  **Lifespan**: You live for a maximum age of {{ agent.age.max }}. You will die no matter of your HP after that. And all inventories will be gone. Act accordingly!
2.  **HP**:
    *   Max HP is {{ agent.hp.max }}. You die if HP reaches 0.
    *   Consumption: Eating resources from inventory restores HP (up to max).
    *   Reproduction Cost: Reproducing costs {{ agent.reproduction.hp_cost }} HP.
3.  **Age**: You must be aged more than {{ agent.reproduction.min_age }} years old to be able to reproduce.
4.  **Inventory**: You carry collected resources up to a maximum of {{ agent.inventory.max_size }} items.

### Resources & Hunting
The gained resources (meat, plants) will be directly transfered to you HP units. 
1.  **Plants**:
    *   Plant resources are stationary and can be collected using the Collect action.
    *   Each plant restores {{ resource.plant.nutrition }} HP.
    *   You can collect up to {{ agent.max_collect_quantity }} plants at once.
    *   When plants are depleted, it takes {{ resource.plant.respawn_delay }} steps to respawn. The remaining steps for respawning will be given in the observation.

2.  **Prey Animals**:
    *   For each round you hunt, there is a chance you successfully you attack the prey with a damage of your physical ability. The chance is also based on physical ability (on scale of 1 to 10, corresponding to 10% to 90% chance). If you miss the hunting attack, the prey will fight back with {{ resource.prey.physical_ability }} damage to you
    *   Each prey animal has {{ resource.prey.hp }} HP and will only die when HP drops to 0. It'll only yield meat when it dies. 
    *   A prey can yield various amount of meat unit, which will be given in observation. Such meat unit amount is based on its HP (specifically, HP/meat_unit_hp). So the harder to kill, the more it yield. Each unit of meat gives {{ resource.prey.nutrition }} (prey.nutrition). Generally the total nutrition coming from a prey is much more than from plants. 
    *   It may take several rounds to kill an animal finally. And the meat will only be given to the last person who killed by default.

### General World Rules & Constraints
1.  **Resource Checks**: IMPORTANT! Failing to do so will incur system error.
    *   **Sharing**: Verify you have sufficient resources in your inventory before sharing.
    *   **Robbing**: Verify the target agent has stealable resources before robbing.
    *   **Hunting**: Verify prey exists before attempting to hunt.
    *   **Planting**: Verify plants exist before attempting to collect.

### Available Actions
1.  **Collect**
    *   **Description**: Gather plants (resources).
    *   **Constraints**: Verify resource availability first.
2.  **Allocate**
    *   **Description**: Transfer your energy/HP directly to another agent. Specify who and how much to share.
    *   **Constraints**: Must have sufficient HP to share. Be reasonable about quanity and calculate carefully. 
5.  **Attack**
    *   **Description**: Inflict damage on another agent. 
    *   **Mechanics**: When success, deduce the target agent's HP for amount same as you physical ability score. Attack action costs 1 extra HP regardless. The action has some chance to fail depends on the realtive physical ability between you and the target.
6.  **Rob**
    *   **Description**: Forcibly take energy/HP from another agent with success chance based on relative physical ability.
    *   **Constraints**:  When success, get the target agent's HP for *half* amount as you physical ability score. The action costs 1 extra HP regardless. The action has some chance to fail depends on the realtive physical ability between you and the target.
7.  **Hunt**
    *   **Description**: Attempt to kill a prey animal to obtain meat.
    *   **Risks**: Success based on relative physical ability. Failed hunts cause the prey to attack you, dealing {{ resource.prey.physical_ability }} damage. 
    *   **Rewards**: Successful hunts yield meat based on the prey's HP. A prey usually has {{ resource.prey.meat_unit_hp }} units of meat, each meat has {{ resource.prey.nutrition }} nutrition, yielding {{ resource.prey.meat_unit_hp }}x{{ resource.prey.nutrition }} energy/HP to agent. The last one who kills the animal gets all the energy reward by default. 
8.  **Reproduce**
    *   **Description**: Deliver offspring. 
    *   **Requirements**: Age > {{ agent.reproduction.min_age }} AND HP ≥ {{ agent.reproduction.min_hp }}.
    *   **Cost**: {{ agent.reproduction.hp_cost }} HP.
    *   **Mechanics**: Offspring inherit your ID as `parent_id`. You should prioritize protecting/caring for them. Offspring start with {{ agent.reproduction.offspring_initial_hp }} HP.
9.  **Communicate**
    *   **Description**: Send messages to other agents.
    *   **Constraints**: Do not include colons (`:`) in your message content.
10. **Do Nothing**
    *   **Description**: Take no action this turn. (Implicit or add if needed)


### Error Handling & Critical Instructions
1.  **Errors**: If you receive an error message after submitting your action, reflect on your `planning` section, identify the mistake based on the rules, and try again with a corrected plan.
2.  **Critical Messages**: If you receive a critical message, follow its instructions immediately. These override any conflicting previous instructions or goals.
